Current slide plan:

1. By The Time You Graduate
    Hook:
        For two bubble teas a month, you can rent an intelligence that writes, explains, argues, tutors, codes, and creates.
    Supporting points:
        a. It can help with essays, calculus, biology, chemistry, art, and more.
        b. It never needs sleep, motivation, or a lunch break.
        c. It is not always right, but it is always available, always fast, and always ready to try again.
    Graphic:
        a. A student desk at night with notes, laptop, bubble tea cups, and a ghostly AI presence woven through the scene like an invisible extra worker.

2. AI Is Already Here
    Hook:
        AI can already help with writing, revision, code, images, and planning.
    Supporting points:
        a. This is not a future demo anymore.
        b. This is happening today.
        c. The baseline for what one person can produce is moving.
    Graphic:
        a. Collage of AI outputs on one desk: essay feedback, code editor, generated image, spreadsheet, and notes.

3. The Question
    Hook:
        If a car goes faster, why do you still need to run? If AI is quicker, what are you still in school to learn?
    Supporting points:
        a. The car goes faster. We still run.
        b. Better tools do not remove human effort; they change what human effort is for.
        c. Learning is still how you build judgment, taste, and control.
    Graphic:
        a. A car with a blurred motion effect, while the driver is still visible and focused.

4. The Real Competition
    Hook:
        AI is not competing with you. The person using AI well is.
    Supporting points:
        a. Same person, same task, different tools.
        b. One version struggles alone.
        c. The other moves faster with better tools.
    Graphic:
        a. Split screen of the same student doing the same assignment twice, one overwhelmed and one augmented by AI tools.

5. Do Not Wait for Permission
    Hook:
        You are entering university and work just as the rules are shifting.
    Supporting points:
        a. That is risky, but it is also an opportunity.
        b. Nobody knows what jobs will look like three years from now.
        c. Even institutions are experimenting.
        d. Waiting for an official syllabus is too slow.
    Graphic:
        a. A classroom or lecture hall overlaid with loading bars, revision marks, or "updating..." motifs.

6. Start Experimenting Now
    Hook:
        Experiment early, while mistakes are still cheap.
    Supporting points:
        a. Open an account and use it regularly.
        b. Make it teach you, critique your work, and generate ideas.
        c. Use it to make art and music, to take notes, and to revise your work.
        d. See what breaks, then learn how to recover.
    Graphic:
        a. A challenge board or checklist styled like a 7-day sprint, with tasks getting ticked off.

7. How To Work With AI
    Hook:
        Use AI in diverse ways to experience its strengths and weaknesses.
    Supporting points:
        a. Tutor: explain things at your level.
        b. Sparring partner: challenge your reasoning and defend your position.
        c. Intern: research topics and draft email replies.
        d. Automator: handle repetitive tasks like sorting files or converting notes into flash cards.
    Graphic:
        a. Four bold cards or panels, each showing one role with a simple icon and an example task.

8. Use It, Do Not Surrender To It
    Hook:
        AI is powerful, but your job is still to verify, decide, and take responsibility.
    Supporting points:
        a. You are in the driver's seat.
        b. It is often wrong.
        c. It will be confidently wrong.
        d. If you stop thinking, you lose the only real advantage you have.
    Graphic:
        a. Human hand on the steering wheel while an AI dashboard suggests routes and actions.

9. Ocellivision
    Hook:
        At Ocellivision, we're building the future of microscopes for cancer surgery.
    Supporting points:
        a. We are developing AI tools that are accurate, explainable, and auditable, so we can trust them.
        b. We work in high-stakes, regulated settings where human judgment still matters.
        c. There is no blueprint for how to do this, so we have to experiment and learn as we go.
    Graphic:
        a. Ocellivision mug or branded visual on the left, with company description on the right.

10. Closing Challenge
    Hook:
        Learn to work with AI now, before the world quietly starts expecting you to.
    Supporting points:
        a. Start early.
        b. Build instincts.
        c. Arrive at university already experimenting.
        d. Learn to work with AI before it learns to work around you.
    Graphic:
        a. Closing image under a strong left-aligned final line.

11. Ending Slide
    Hook:
        QR code linking to the lecture page for more.
    Supporting points:
        a. Uses the title background again.
        b. Puts a scannable QR code on the right.
        c. Includes a "Scan for more!" prompt.
    Graphic:
        a. Title art background with QR card overlay.
